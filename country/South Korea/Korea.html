South Korea

South Korea is a country in East Asia, constituting the southern part of the Korean Peninsula.The name Korea is derived from Goguryeo which was one of the great powers in East Asia during its time uling most of the Korean Peninsula, Manchuria, parts of the Russian Far East and Inner Mongolia under Gwanggaeto the Great.

Seoul

Seoul largest metropolis of South Korea. Half of South Korea's over 51 million people live in the Seoul Capital Area. In 2015, it was rated Asia's most livable city with the second highest quality of life globally by Arcadis. In 2017, the cost of living in Seoul was ranked 6th globally. In 2018, Seoul's real estate market was ranked 5th in the world for the price of apartments in the downtown center. The city has been known in the past by the names Wiryeseong.

Interesting Facts

  • Population:51 million people live in South Korea (2017). The majority of the people live in the province surrounding Seoul in the north west of the country.
  • National Holiday: 15 August (Liberation Day)
  • Literacy: More than 95% can read and write
  • Currency: 1 South Korean Won equals 100 Jeon
  • National Symbols: taegeuk (yin yang symbol), Hibiscus syriacus (national flower) and the Siberian tiger (national animal). The national colours are red, white, blue and black.
  • Language: Children learn Mandarin and English in schools and most people speak English fairly well.

It was long believed that the Korean people originally may have had links with the people of Central Asia, the Lake Baikal region of Siberia, Mongolia, and the coastal areas of the Yellow Sea. Tools of Paleolithic type and other artifacts found in Sokch’ang, near Kongju, are quite similar to those of the Lake Baikal and Mongolian areas. In 2017, genetic analysis of bones found in Primorye kray in Far Eastern Russia suggested that Koreans were related to a population that had inhabited that area for at least 7,700 years. The genes of these Neolithic humans were expressed alongside those of indigenous agriculturalists from Southeast Asia to produce the genetic structure of modern Koreans.
